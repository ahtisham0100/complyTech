## 2. Competitor Profiles (Deep Dive)

### Competitor 1 — accessiBe
**Domain:** accessibe.com | **Type:** AI Overlay SaaS

**What They Do:**
A JavaScript widget you add to your website in one line. Claims to automatically remediate accessibility barriers via AI.

**Strengths:**
- Extremely easy to sell ("install in 2 minutes")
- Low price point ($49–$490/mo) with high volume
- Strong brand awareness and heavy advertising spend

**Critical Weaknesses (Your Opportunities):**
- **FTC enforcement order in 2025** — penalized for deceptive "full compliance" claims
- Companies using accessiBe **continue to receive lawsuits** — overlay does not fix underlying code
- National Federation of the Blind, American Council of the Blind, and Disability Rights Advocates have all formally condemned overlay products
- Cannot test keyboard navigation, screen readers, dynamic content, or cognitive accessibility
- Detects ~20–30% of WCAG issues at best

**SEO/GEO Position:**
- Ranks well for "ADA compliance website" brand terms
- Heavy PPC spending — high CPCs on "ADA compliance" keywords
- **GEO weakness:** AI systems increasingly cite studies showing overlays are legally insufficient — a trend that creates organic citation opportunity for complyTech

**complyTech Battlecard vs. accessiBe:**
> "accessiBe installs in 2 minutes and costs $49/month. In 2025, the FTC penalized the company for deceptive compliance claims. Companies using accessiBe have continued to be successfully sued. A 2-minute JavaScript widget cannot replace a 5–14 day manual audit by IAAP-certified specialists."

### Competitor 2 — AudioEye
**Domain:** audioeye.com | **Type:** Platform + Managed Services

**What They Do:**
Hybrid model — automated monitoring platform plus human-assisted remediation services. Positioned between overlay and full manual audit.

**Strengths:**
- Public company (AEYE on Nasdaq) — strong brand credibility
- Offers both automated and human-assisted components
- Strong content marketing presence

**Weaknesses:**
- Pricing is hidden (requires demo/sales call)
- SMB entry pricing starts with automated features, not full manual audit
- Sales process too long for small businesses seeking quick quotes
- Customer reviews cite unclear deliverables and upsell pressure

**SEO/Content:**
- Ranks for informational ADA content
- Strong blog presence targeting "ADA compliance" educational keywords
- **Gap:** Weak specific content for healthcare, small business with transparent pricing

**complyTech Battlecard vs. AudioEye:**
> "AudioEye requires a sales demo before revealing pricing. complyTech publishes transparent pricing starting at $495 and responds within 1 business day via WhatsApp. No upsell pressure — your quote includes scope, timeline, and pricing upfront."

### Competitor 3 — Level Access / UserWay
**Domain:** levelaccess.com | **Type:** Enterprise Platform

**What They Do:**
The most technically rigorous platform in the market. Deep manual testing, SDLC integration, enterprise governance. Acquired UserWay in 2023.

**Strengths:**
- Gold standard for enterprise compliance
- IAAP-certified internal team
- Strong government and Fortune 500 client base
- Most complete audit methodology in the industry

**Weaknesses:**
- Pricing starts at $15,000+ — completely inaccessible for the SMB market
- Complex sales process (months of procurement)
- UserWay acquisition created confusion in their SMB positioning
- Not designed for solo business owners or micro-businesses

**SEO/Content:**
- Dominates enterprise keywords
- **Gap:** No useful content or service for "ADA compliance audit for small business" at accessible price points. Their content exists but their services don't serve this audience.

**complyTech Battlecard vs. Level Access:**
> "Level Access starts at $15,000 — built for Fortune 500 enterprises. complyTech delivers the same IAAP-certified manual audit methodology starting at $1,500 for small businesses, with deliverables in 5–14 days, not months."

### Competitor 4 — Deque Systems
**Domain:** deque.com | **Type:** Enterprise Agency + Dev Tools

**What They Do:**
Industry pioneers of axe-core (the accessibility testing library). Focus on development team integration, enterprise auditing, and DevOps pipelines.

**Weaknesses:**

- Developer-focused, not business-owner-focused communication

**complyTech Battlecard vs. Deque:**

Same methodology, a fraction of the price, and you'll actually understand the report."

### Competitor 5 — Freelancers (Upwork / Fiverr)
**Type:** Independent contractors

**What They Do:**
Individual accessibility consultants offering manual WCAG audits on freelance platforms.

**Strengths:**
- Low price point ($500–$3,000)
- More accessible than enterprise vendors

**Weaknesses:**
- Highly variable quality — no consistent VPAT deliverable standard
- No business entity trust signal (no agency, no liability)
- No compliance certificate, re-test, or post-audit support
- Difficult to vet IAAP certification claims
- No specialized healthcare or Section 1557 knowledge

**complyTech Battlecard vs. Freelancers:**
> "A freelancer on Upwork costs less but delivers inconsistently. complyTech is a professional agency with IAAP-certified auditors, standardized VPAT deliverables, a compliance certificate post-remediation, and WhatsApp-accessible support throughout the process."
